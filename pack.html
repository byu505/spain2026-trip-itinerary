What to pack

Based on weather and the itinerary.

Home Travel Itinerary Hotels Weather What to pack

Late May in Sevilla and Madrid: warm days (high 70s–80s °F), cooler evenings (mid 50s–60s °F). Lots of walking, museums, tapas, and maybe one slightly dressier evening. No formal dress code required.

Clothing

  • Tops — Short sleeves and a few long-sleeve or light layers for evening and air‑conditioned museums.
  • Bottoms — Light pants, shorts, or skirts; comfortable for all-day walking.
  • One warmer layer — Light jacket, cardigan, or fleece for cool evenings and early morning.
  • One slightly nicer outfit — Optional for a nice dinner or rooftop bar (smart casual is enough).
  • Underwear & socks — Enough for the trip or plan to do a quick wash; moisture-wicking helps in the heat.

Shoes

  • Walking shoes — Broken-in sneakers or comfortable sandals. You’ll walk a lot (Alcázar, Cathedral, Retiro, Gran Vía).
  • Second pair — Sandals or a second pair to rotate; useful if one gets wet or for evening.

Sun & heat

  • Sunglasses
  • Hat or cap — For long stints outside (Plaza de España, Retiro, rooftop bars).
  • Sunscreen — SPF 30+; strong sun in both cities.
  • Reusable water bottle — Stay hydrated; you can refill at many spots.

Documents & money

  • Passport — Valid for at least 3 months beyond your stay (EU rule).
  • Phone & charger — Consider a small portable battery for long days out.
  • Cards & some cash — Cards widely accepted; a bit of euro cash useful for small places and tips.
  • Travel/health insurance info — If you have it, keep details handy.

Optional (itinerary-based)

  • Small day bag — For museum visits, water, sunscreen, and layers.
  • Earphones — For train (Sevilla → Madrid) or flights.
  • Adapter — Spain uses Type F (EU) outlets; if coming from US/UK, bring an adapter.
  • Medications — Any personal meds; basic pain reliever and stomach aid can be handy.
